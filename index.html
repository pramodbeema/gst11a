Analysis of Section 11A of CGST Act, 2017

Amid the prevailing negativity and controversy surrounding the taxation burden on the middle class, Union Finance Minister Smt. Nirmala Sitharaman has introduced a significant amendment through The Finance Act (No. 2), 2024.

¬†

This amendment aims to provide relief to small and micro-scale business owners who do not habitually collect Goods and Services Tax (GST) on their supplies.

¬†

Section 11A. Power not to recover Goods and Services Tax not levied or short-levied as a result of general practice.

Understanding the Provision

To better understand the impact of Section 11A, let's consider an example of a small panipuri vendor.

¬†

Imagine a panipuri vendor who has been running his small stall for years, unaware that he was supposed to collect and pay GST on his sales. Due to the general practice in his locality, most vendors like him do not charge GST, as they believe their business falls outside the tax net. However, without this new provision, tax authorities could later audit his earnings and demand a large sum in back taxes, penalties, and interest. This unexpected liability could cripple his business financially, potentially forcing him to shut down.

¬†

With the introduction of Section 11A, the government acknowledges such commonly followed practices and offers relief by waiving the retrospective tax demand. If the government determines that not collecting GST was a prevalent practice in his industry and region, it can issue a notification to exempt him from paying the back taxes. This provision provides much-needed protection and assurance, allowing small business owners to continue their operations without the fear of sudden financial liabilities.

¬†

In essence, the absence of this section could place a heavy burden on small vendors due to their lack of awareness and formal financial knowledge. On the other hand, its presence provides legal protection and flexibility, encouraging compliance without undue hardship.

Conclusion

The introduction of Section 11A offers much-needed relief to small and micro business owners who may not have been aware of their GST obligations due to common business practices. It underscores the government's commitment to protecting small entrepreneurs from undue financial burdens while maintaining tax compliance in a fair and transparent manner.
